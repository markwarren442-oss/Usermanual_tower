Project Overview

This manual serves as the primary reference for the Design and Fabrication of a Modular Tower Model for Structural Analysis.

📋 Note

This model uses a high-precision interlocking system (LEGO) to allow civil engineering students to physically manipulate variables.

Project Objectives

  • 1

    Develop Interactive Model

    To develop and utilize an interactive LEGO structural model that helps students understand fundamental structural engineering concepts through hands-on construction and experimentation.

  • 2

    Demonstrate Behavior

    To demonstrate real-life structural behavior by constructing models that represent common engineering structures such as towers, frames, and braced systems.

  • 3

    Skill Development

    To enhance problem-solving, critical thinking, teamwork, and collaboration skills by allowing students to experiment with different structural designs.

Fabrication Procedure

Procedure of Making the Tower.

⚠️ Construction Note

Construct the tower in detachable sections so that each part can be assembled and disassembled easily during presentation.

Step-by-step Fabrication

  • 1

    Setup & Preparation

    Prepare all required materials such as balloon sticks, paste/adhesive, cutter, ruler, measuring tools, and the proposed tower design. Measure and cut the balloon sticks according to the required lengths for the base, columns, braces, and upper sections.

  • 2

    Base Construction

    Build the base first by forming a wide and stable foundation. Ensure all joints are firmly attached and aligned properly.

  • 3

    Vertical & Horizontal Assembly

    Assemble the vertical members or columns upward from the base, followed by horizontal members and diagonal bracing to increase strength and stability.

  • 4

    Upper Sections & Reinforcement

    Continue constructing the middle and upper portions of the tower, gradually reducing the width as the height increases based on the design. Allow each connected section to dry properly before attaching the next part. Inspect all sections and reinforce weak joints.

  • 5

    Storage & Transport

    Create a storage box inspired by a chessboard-type container, with partitions or spaces intended for each detachable tower section. Arrange and place all disassembled tower parts inside the box properly.

Expected Outcomes

The anticipated educational impacts of the model.

Educational Value

Primary Outcome The expected outcome of this study is the successful development of an interactive LEGO tower model that will help students better understand fundamental structural engineering concepts such as stability, load distribution, balance, and structural behavior.
Long-term Impact The LEGO tower model is expected to serve as a simple yet effective educational tool that demonstrates real-life structural behavior, promotes experiential learning, and increases students’ interest and appreciation for structural and civil engineering concepts.
